Title: XI Chrysalis Pain (from Here, Yesterday)

My father was a remarkably good and special person, who lived for my mother and myself.

So, when, all of a sudden, he got sick

we both were at the verge of a nervous breakdown.

Sick as he was, he stayed home, behind the door.

Seated in one of the old chairs we had.

Bless him!

Not even a rocker he had to rest his tired body!

Like a dried flower that has lost all chances to live

that becomes dust to the touch, as every root, petal and leaf crumble.

Sad and wither, as a silent whisper

same I felt, clinging to the memory of my father like the prey flees its hunter.

Like a violent stream pulverising after a waterfall.

Like a thorn of a rose, too deep into the skin.

Night after night, for two months, I dreamed about him. Unitl the dim light of the firefly was no more.

Details: Pencil over paper and digital colour, 800x430mm, 2022
